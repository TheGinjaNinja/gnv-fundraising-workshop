FieldFlow has pilot customers generating revenue, putting it ahead of most pre-seed startups. But revenue is small, customer count is low, and the acquisition channel is not yet repeatable.

Key Strengths

Founder-problem fit is genuine. Arben ran a trades business for 12 years. He is not guessing at the problem. He lived it.
Working product with paying customers. At pre-seed, 3 paying customers with zero churn is a strong signal. Most startups at this stage have mockups and waitlists.
Capital efficiency. The team has achieved a lot on very little. This signals resourcefulness and operational discipline.

Key Gaps

Unit economics are unproven. CAC, LTV, and payback period are all estimates. The first 3 customers came through personal network.
Business model is early. EUR 29/month works for 3 customers but has not been stress-tested at scale.
Competitive advantage relies on speed. Being first in the Western Balkans is an advantage today, but it is not a moat.

Hook Selection

"Last month, a plumber in Pristina lost EUR 2,400 because three jobs fell through the cracks of his paper diary."

The story version beats the question alternative ("What if your business lost EUR 2,400 because of a paper diary?") because it names a real person in a real place with a real loss. Specific stories create more emotional connection than hypothetical questions.

Top 3 Prepared Q&A

"Why trades businesses?"

Arben ran one for 12 years. He lost EUR 15K in a single year to scheduling mistakes alone. He did not read a market report and decide this was interesting. He lived the problem, got fed up, and built the solution he wished he had.

"What about ServiceTitan or Jobber?"

ServiceTitan and Jobber serve English-speaking markets at EUR 200+ per month for businesses with 20+ employees. Nobody serves small trades businesses in the Western Balkans at EUR 29/month in Albanian, Serbian, or Macedonian. Different customer, different price point, different language.

"How do you acquire customers?"

Arben visits trade associations and building supply shops in person. Eight of our 12 interview contacts came through two introductions at a hardware store in Pristina. The acquisition channel is founder-led today, which is appropriate at pre-seed. Part of this raise funds a test of paid channels to find a repeatable motion.

Action Plan

Next Steps

1
Validate unit economics. Track CAC through a non-network acquisition channel. Run a 4-week paid acquisition experiment targeting trades businesses through Facebook and trade association partnerships. Timeline: next 8 weeks.
2
Test annual plan conversion. Offer new customers a 3-month trial then annual conversion to measure willingness to commit. Ongoing with each new customer.
3
Validate market sizing. Survey 20 trades business owners on current tool spend to move the EUR 50-100/month assumption from Weak to Moderate. Timeline: next 4 weeks.
4
Build competitive advantage. Aggregate anonymised job and pricing data across customers to create benchmarking tools that improve with scale. Start after reaching 20 customers.

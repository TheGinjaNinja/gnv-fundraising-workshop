The Pitch

Pitch Narrative

Positioning

Archetype: Hair on Fire + Dominate a Subsegment

FieldFlow targets a burning, everyday problem for an underserved segment. Trades businesses in the Western Balkans are running on paper diaries and manual invoicing. The opportunity is to own this subsegment completely before expanding.

Teaser Deck

Not Started

5 to 10 slides. This is the deck that gets you the meeting. It gets attached to cold emails and LinkedIn messages. It must not contain sensitive data, detailed financials, or anything you would not want forwarded to a stranger. Its only job is to make an investor curious enough to book a call.

Slides to include:

  1. Hook . The opening line that makes them stop scrolling.
  2. Problem . What is broken and for whom.
  3. Solution . What you built, in one visual.
  4. Traction . The numbers that prove it is working.
  5. Ask . How much you are raising and what it funds.

Reading Deck

Not Started

15 to 20 slides. This is the deck that stands alone when forwarded. It is what gets sent after a meeting, or forwarded by an associate to a partner. Every claim has context. Every chart has a label. Every number has a source. A reader who has never met you should be able to understand your business, your traction, and your ask without anyone talking them through it.

This deck includes everything from the presenting deck plus detailed market analysis, competitive positioning, unit economics, team bios, financial projections, and appendix materials.

Cold Email Template

Subject: Trades businesses losing 15-20% revenue to paper chaos
Hi [First name], [Why them: 1-2 sentences referencing their portfolio, thesis, or a specific investment. 15 minutes of research per email.] FieldFlow is a mobile app that replaces paper diaries, spreadsheets, and manual invoicing for trades businesses in the Western Balkans. Our 3 pilot customers cut invoicing time from 3 days to 30 seconds. Key signals: - CEO ran a trades business for 12 years, CTO built scheduling systems at a logistics company in Zurich - Live product in daily use by 3 paying customers - EUR 840/month MRR, zero churn in 4 months - 94% feature usage across all modules Raising EUR 150K pre-seed. Selected for [accelerator programme]. Would you be open to a 30-minute call next week? Arben Krasniqi CEO, FieldFlow

Cold Email Rules

  • Personal greeting. Use their first name. Never "Dear Sir/Madam" or "To whom it may concern."
  • "Why them" is non-negotiable. Reference their portfolio, thesis, or a specific investment they have made. This line proves you did the work. Generic emails get deleted.
  • Value prop in one sentence. What you do, for whom, and what result you deliver. No jargon.
  • Positive signals as bullets. Names and numbers only. Investor-relevant proof points, not a feature list.
  • Clear call to action. Ask for a specific next step. "30-minute call next week" is better than "would love to connect sometime."
  • Keep it short. The entire email should fit on a phone screen without scrolling. If you need more space, your teaser deck does the heavy lifting.

Hook Selection

"Last month, a plumber in Pristina lost EUR 2,400 because three jobs fell through the cracks of his paper diary."

Why this works: It is specific (one person, one city, one amount). It names a real consequence of a real problem. It is emotionally relatable. Every investor has hired a tradesperson and knows the chaos. It sets up the solution perfectly without giving it away.

Investor Q&A Preparation

Question Prepared Answer
"Why the Western Balkans? Is this market big enough?" 50,000+ registered trades businesses in Kosovo alone. The market is underserved by technology. We start here because we know it deeply, then expand regionally. The playbook is proven: Gojek started in Jakarta, Grab started in Malaysia.
"3 customers is not exactly traction. How do you scale?" 3 paying customers with zero churn and 94% feature usage is exceptional product-market fit signal at pre-seed. Our next milestone is 25 customers in 12 months through trade association partnerships and word of mouth within trades networks.
"What stops a bigger player from copying this?" The global players (Jobber, ServiceTitan) do not localise for Balkan markets. They do not support local payment systems, local languages, or local invoicing requirements. Our moat is deep local knowledge and a product built specifically for this market.
"Why is the CEO the right person for this?" Arben ran a trades business for 12 years. He has lived this problem. He knows the customers, the workflows, and the pain points because they were his pain points. That domain expertise is why our feature usage is at 94%.
"EUR 150K seems small. Is that enough?" Burn rate in Kosovo is significantly lower than Western Europe. EUR 150K gives us 12 months of runway to reach 25 paying customers and EUR 7K MRR. That puts us in a strong position for a seed round with real traction data.

Prioritised Open Actions

  1. Build the teaser deck. 5 slides: hook, problem, solution, traction, ask. Attach to every cold email. No sensitive data.
  2. Source credible market data. The market slide is the weakest element. Find regional statistics on trades businesses in Kosovo and the Western Balkans. Eurostat, local chambers of commerce, World Bank data.
  3. Write 10 personalised cold emails. Use the template above. Spend 15 minutes researching each investor before writing the "why them" line.
  4. Build a target investor list of 30+ names. Prioritise diaspora angels and accelerator networks. Research who has invested in Balkan startups before.
  5. Sharpen the use of proceeds. Break down the EUR 150K into specific milestones with timelines. Investors want to see exactly what their money buys.
  6. Build the reading deck. 15 to 20 slides that stand alone. Add detailed market analysis, competitive positioning, unit economics, and team bios.
  7. Practice the 3-minute pitch 20 times. Record yourself. Watch it back. Get the timing locked so the hook, traction, and ask all land at the right moments.

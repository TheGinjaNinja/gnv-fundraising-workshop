## Signal Assessment

**Signal Level:** Decent Signal

FieldFlow has pilot customers generating revenue, which puts it ahead of most pre-seed startups. But the revenue is small (EUR 840/month MRR), the customer count is low (3), and the acquisition channel is not repeatable. Investors will see potential but will negotiate hard on valuation.

---

## Key Strengths

- **Founder-problem fit is genuine.** Arben ran a trades business for 12 years. He is not guessing at the problem. He lived it.
- **Working product with paying customers.** At pre-seed, having 3 paying customers with zero churn is a strong signal. Most startups at this stage have mockups and waitlists.
- **Capital efficiency.** The team has achieved a lot on very little. This signals resourcefulness and operational discipline.

## Key Gaps

- **Unit economics are unproven.** CAC, LTV, and payback period are all estimates. The first 3 customers came through Arben's personal network. Repeatable acquisition has not been tested.
- **Business model is early.** EUR 29/month works for 3 customers but has not been stress-tested. There is no evidence on price sensitivity, willingness to pay for annual plans at scale, or expansion revenue potential.
- **Competitive advantage relies on speed, not defensibility.** Being first in the Western Balkans is an advantage today, but it is not a moat. The team needs to build network effects or data advantages before a larger player enters the market.

## Action Plan

| Gap | Action | Timeline |
|-----|--------|----------|

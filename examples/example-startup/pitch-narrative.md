# Exercise 1: Pitch Narrative

**Startup:** FieldFlow
**Date:** 2026-04-09
**Stage:** Pre-seed
**One-liner:** Mobile-first job management for small trades businesses in the Western Balkans.

---

|-------|--------------|-------------|----------|

This is a healthy evidence profile for a pre-seed startup. The core product and traction claims are strong. Market sizing and vision need further validation.

---

## Positioning

**Sequoia Archetype:** Hair on Fire. Trades businesses are actively losing money to scheduling chaos. They know they have a problem. They are not waiting to be educated about it. They are looking for something that works.

**Dunford Positioning:** Dominate a Subsegment. ServiceTitan (US, EUR 200+/month) and Jobber (North America, English-only) serve large trades businesses in developed markets. Nobody serves small trades businesses in the Western Balkans at EUR 29/month in local languages. FieldFlow owns this niche.

**Market Framing:** The Western Balkans is digitising rapidly. Smartphone penetration in Kosovo crossed 85% in 2025. Trades businesses have the hardware in their pockets but no software built for how they work. This is a timing opportunity.

---

## Story Arc

**Selected:** Demo Day Arc (default). This is a Hair on Fire problem with strong traction evidence. The standard structure works well. No need for a Strategic Narrative (no macro shift to explain) or Market Framing (no crowded competitive landscape to navigate).

**Hook:** "Last month, a plumber in Pristina lost EUR 2,400 because three jobs fell through the cracks of his paper diary."

**Ask:** "We are raising EUR 150K to reach 50 paying customers in 12 months."

---

## Next Steps

1. **Validate market sizing.** The EUR 50-100/month spend figure comes from an industry report, not primary research. Survey 20 trades business owners on current tool spend to move this from Weak to Moderate.
2. **Test pricing in Albania.** The EUR 29/month price point works in Pristina but may need adjustment for lower-income markets. Run 5 discovery calls with Albanian trades owners.
3. **Build the expansion case.** The vision slide is pure assumption. Identify one data point that supports cross-border demand (e.g. a trades business owner in Tirana who has heard of FieldFlow through word of mouth).

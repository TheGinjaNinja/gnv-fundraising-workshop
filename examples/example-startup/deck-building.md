# Exercise 2: Deck Building

**Startup:** FieldFlow
**Date:** 2026-04-09
**Format:** Presenting Deck (10 slides, 3-minute demo day pitch)

---

## Slide-by-Slide Outline

### Slide 1: Hook

**Key Narrative:** A real trades business owner is losing real money because of paper-based chaos.
**Content:** "Last month, a plumber in Pristina lost EUR 2,400 because three jobs fell through the cracks of his paper diary."
**Proof Points:** Real story from Driton, one of our pilot customers. He tracked his missed jobs during his first week on FieldFlow. **[Strong]**
**So What:** The problem is tangible, specific, and expensive. The audience feels it immediately.

### Slide 2: Problem

**Key Narrative:** Trades businesses across the Western Balkans lose 15-20% of revenue to scheduling errors, missed follow-ups, and late invoicing.
**Content:** 73% of trades businesses have no digital tools. They run on paper diaries, WhatsApp messages, and memory. Average revenue leakage: 15-20% from missed appointments, forgotten quotes, and invoices sent days late.
**Proof Points:** 12 discovery interviews with trades business owners in Kosovo. Revenue leakage validated against 3 pilot customers' records. **[Moderate]**
**So What:** This is not a nice-to-have. These businesses are bleeding money they cannot afford to lose.

### Slide 3: Solution

**Key Narrative:** FieldFlow replaces five tools with one app. Time from job completion to invoice: 30 seconds.
**Content:** One mobile app for scheduling, customer management, quoting, and invoicing. Works offline. Built for tradespeople, not office workers. Before FieldFlow: job done, drive home, remember to write the invoice two days later, chase payment for two weeks. After FieldFlow: job done, tap the screen, invoice sent, customer pays.
**Proof Points:** Invoicing time measured in pilot: from 2.8 days average to under 1 minute. **[Strong]**
**So What:** Faster invoicing means faster payment. Trades businesses get paid days sooner with zero extra effort.

### Slide 4: Product

**Key Narrative:** A working product that trades people actually use every day.
**Content:** Screenshots of the mobile app: job scheduling calendar, one-tap invoicing, customer timeline. Built in Flutter for Android and iOS. Offline-capable for on-site use in areas with poor connectivity.
**Proof Points:** Live product in daily use by 3 pilot customers. 94% feature usage across all modules. **[Strong]**
**So What:** This is not a prototype or a mockup. It is a product that real trades people rely on daily.

### Slide 5: Market

**Key Narrative:** 48,000 trades businesses across three countries, with no digital tools built for them.
**Content:** TAM: 48,000 registered trades businesses in Kosovo, Albania, and North Macedonia. Average tool spend: EUR 50-100/month. SAM: EUR 28.8M. SOM (Year 1, Kosovo only): 500 businesses at EUR 29/month = EUR 174K ARR.
**Proof Points:** Business registration data from national statistics agencies. Spend estimate from industry association report. **[Weak]**
**So What:** The market is underserved and quantifiable. The spend assumption needs validation, but the customer base is real and countable.

### Slide 6: Business Model

**Key Narrative:** SaaS subscription at EUR 29/month per business. Simple, predictable, scalable.
**Content:** Monthly plan: EUR 29. Annual plan: EUR 279 (20% discount). One plan, one price. No tiering complexity at this stage. Current MRR: EUR 840 from 3 customers.
**Proof Points:** 3 pilot customers paying monthly. One converted to annual after 3 months. **[Moderate]**
**So What:** Willingness to pay is proven. The model is simple enough to scale without a sales team.

### Slide 7: Traction

**Key Narrative:** 3 paying customers, EUR 840/month MRR, zero churn in 4 months.
**Content:** All three pilot customers are trades businesses in Pristina (2 plumbers, 1 electrician). Average feature usage: 94% across all modules. Customer satisfaction: all three have referred FieldFlow to at least one peer. Net promoter score: not formally measured, but organic referral behaviour is strong.
**Proof Points:** Payment records, analytics dashboard, retention data. **[Strong]**
**So What:** At pre-seed, 3 paying customers with high engagement and zero churn is the strongest signal possible. These are not beta testers. They are paying customers who chose to stay.

### Slide 8: Team

**Key Narrative:** A tradesperson who lived the problem and an engineer who has built exactly this type of system.
**Content:** Arben Krasniqi (CEO): 12 years as a licensed electrician in Pristina. Ran his own 4-person business. Lost EUR 15K in one year to scheduling mistakes. Lira Berisha (CTO): 6 years engineering experience. Built scheduling and dispatch systems at a logistics company in Zurich. Returned to Kosovo to build FieldFlow.
**Proof Points:** Direct domain experience. Arben is the customer. Lira has built the technology before. **[Strong]**
**So What:** Founder-problem fit does not get stronger than this. Arben understands the customer because he was one.

### Slide 9: Vision

**Key Narrative:** Start with trades in Kosovo. Become the operating platform for trades businesses across the Western Balkans and beyond.
**Content:** Phase 1 (Year 1): 50 paying customers in Kosovo. Phase 2 (Year 2): expand to Albania and North Macedonia. Phase 3 (Year 3-4): platform play with integrated payments, materials procurement, and insurance partnerships. Long-term: the trades business operating system for emerging European markets.
**Proof Points:** No evidence for expansion. This is the founders' strategic hypothesis. **[Assumption]**
**So What:** The initial market is small but the expansion path is logical. Each new country uses the same platform with localised language and payment integration.

### Slide 10: The Ask

**Key Narrative:** EUR 150K for 12 months of runway. Clear milestones, modest burn, specific outcomes.
**Content:** Raising EUR 150K. Use of funds: hire 1 engineer (EUR 72K), customer acquisition across Kosovo (EUR 36K), Albania pricing validation (EUR 12K), operations and buffer (EUR 30K). Milestones: 50 paying customers, EUR 1,450/month MRR, validated pricing in a second market.
**Proof Points:** Burn rate projection: EUR 11K/month. Runway: 13.6 months. CAC estimate: EUR 120 per customer based on pilot acquisition costs. **[Moderate]**
**So What:** This is a capital-efficient raise in a low-cost geography. EUR 150K buys meaningful traction, not just runway.

## Deck Notes

- This is a presenting deck. Minimal text on slides. The spoken narrative carries the story.
- Slides 1-3 establish the emotional arc: pain, scale, relief.
- Slides 4-7 build the business case: product, market, model, proof.
- Slides 8-10 close with confidence: the right team, a big vision, a clear ask.
- The weakest slide is Market (Slide 5). The spend assumption needs primary research. Acknowledge this if asked, and explain the validation plan.

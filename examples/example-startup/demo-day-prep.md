## Prepared Q&A (30 seconds each)

**1. "Why trades businesses?"**
Arben ran one for 12 years. He lost EUR 15K in a single year to scheduling mistakes alone. He did not read a market report and decide this was interesting. He lived the problem, got fed up, and built the solution he wished he had.

**2. "What about ServiceTitan or Jobber?"**
ServiceTitan and Jobber serve English-speaking markets at EUR 200+ per month. They are built for trades businesses with 20+ employees and back-office staff. Nobody serves small trades businesses in the Western Balkans at EUR 29/month in Albanian, Serbian, or Macedonian. Different customer, different price point, different language.

**3. "How do you acquire customers?"**
Arben visits trade associations and building supply shops in person. Eight of our 12 interview contacts came through two introductions at a hardware store in Pristina. The acquisition channel is founder-led today, which is appropriate at pre-seed. Part of this raise funds a test of paid channels to find a repeatable motion.

**4. "Can this scale across borders?"**
The platform is built in Flutter with a localisation layer. Adding a new market means translating the interface, integrating a local payment provider, and adjusting tax templates. Not rebuilding the product. The architecture was designed for multi-market from day one because Lira built multi-region systems at her previous company.

**5. "What if you do not raise?"**
We continue with our 3 pilot customers, grow to 10 organically through referrals, and re-approach investors with stronger traction in 6 months. Arben and Lira are both working on FieldFlow full-time and have personal runway for 8 more months. We are not raising because we are desperate. We are raising because EUR 150K accelerates what is already working.

## Hook Selection

**Chosen hook:** "Last month, a plumber in Pristina lost EUR 2,400 because three jobs fell through the cracks of his paper diary."

**Alternative tested:** "What if your business lost EUR 2,400 because of a paper diary?"

**Why the story wins:** The question version is abstract. It asks the audience to imagine something. The story version is concrete. It names a real person in a real place with a real loss. Specific stories create more emotional connection than hypothetical questions. The audience pictures Driton. They feel the frustration. Then they want to know what happened next.

Problem

The Accidental CFO

B2B SaaS founders need CFO thinking earlier than any other type of startup. MRR, deferred revenue, burn multiples, R&D tax credits. The business model is finance-heavy from day one. But a real CFO costs GBP 100K+ a year. A fractional CFO costs GBP 1-3K a month. Both are out of reach before seed.

So founders muddle through. They set up the wrong bank accounts. They break invoicing. They overspend without seeing it. They cannot produce accurate reports. They cannot forecast ahead. They arrive at investor meetings unable to answer basic questions about their own numbers.

The cost of getting this wrong is the company.

Market Insight

Existing tools record. They do not think.

Xero and QuickBooks are accounting tools. They record what happened. They do not tell a founder what it means, what to do about it, or what is coming next. Founders need judgement, not bookkeeping. They need a CFO, not a ledger. Until now, that judgement was only available to companies that could afford to hire one.

Solution

CFO-grade clarity from first revenue

Ginger Bread gives UK B2B SaaS founders the financial thinking they need from the day they have customers. Three things change:

Visibility

Know exactly how much money you are spending and where.

Instead of losing sleep at night guessing.

Decisions

Say yes to a new hire in the morning because you know to the day when the money runs out.

Instead of delaying for six weeks because you are too scared to run the numbers.

Investor Credibility

Answer "what's your burn multiple?" in ten seconds without blushing.

Instead of muttering "I'll follow up by email" and losing the room.

Next Steps

Prioritised actions to upgrade evidence ratings before pitching to investors. Each action turns an Assumption into a Moderate, or a Moderate into a Strong.

1
Run 5 pilot conversations. Show the prototype to 5 B2B SaaS founders in the sweet spot (first revenue, doing their own finances). Ask if they would pay GBP 149/month. Ask for a pilot commitment. Three signed pilots upgrade the Traction slide from Moderate to Strong. Timeline: next 2 weeks.
2
Incorporate the company. Cannot raise SEIS without a UK limited company. This is blocking the entire raise. Do this immediately.
3
Apply for SEIS advance assurance. Apply as soon as incorporated. Takes 6-8 weeks from HMRC. Investors will ask for it. Start now.
4
Build a financial model. Monthly spreadsheet: customer acquisition, churn assumption, revenue build, cash balance. You cannot pitch an AI CFO without a model of your own business. Timeline: next week.
5
Verify market sizing. Beauhurst or Dealroom lookup for UK B2B SaaS company count at pre-seed and seed stage. Upgrades Market from Assumption to Moderate. Timeline: next 2 weeks.
6
Test pricing with 5 founders. Ask target founders what they would pay for CFO-grade clarity. Do not lead with a price. Their answer upgrades Business Model from Assumption to Moderate. Timeline: alongside pilot conversations.
7
Source a fractional CFO cost benchmark. IBISWorld or direct quotes from fractional CFO providers. Gives the Problem slide a verifiable comparison point. Timeline: next 2 weeks.
8
Find one founder horror story. A specific example of a founder who lost money or missed a milestone due to bad finance management. With permission. Upgrades the Hook from Moderate to Strong. Ongoing.

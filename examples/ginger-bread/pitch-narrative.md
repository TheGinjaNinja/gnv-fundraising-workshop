### Hook

**Key Narrative:** "Every founder becomes their own CFO the day they incorporate. None of them are qualified. Most of them know it. And right now, there's nothing built for them. We're letting founders make million-pound decisions with spreadsheets, bank apps, and hope. Ginger Bread changes that."

**Proof Points:** The hook is a promise the deck proves. Problem slide validates "none of them are qualified". Product slide validates "nothing built for them". Solution slide validates "Ginger Bread changes that".

**So What?** 15 seconds. Two beats. The room is listening.

**Evidence Rating:** Moderate (grounded in real discovery conversations and founder experience)

---

### Problem

**Key Narrative:** B2B SaaS founders need CFO thinking earlier than any other type of startup because the business model is finance-heavy from day one. MRR vs GAAP revenue, deferred revenue, burn multiples, R&D tax credits. But a real CFO is out of reach until Series A. So founders muddle through, misread their numbers, burn runway faster than they realise, and arrive at investor meetings unable to defend their metrics. The cost of getting this wrong is the company.

**Proof Points:**
- 10 founder discovery conversations surfaced consistent pain around visibility, reporting, and forecasting (Moderate)
- Founders describe feeling "overwhelmed, confused, frustrated" with finances (Moderate)
- Specific mistakes reported: wrong bank account setup, broken invoicing, overspend with no visibility, no accurate reports, no forecasts (Moderate)
- Hiring a real CFO is out of reach at pre-seed and seed, typical CFO salary GBP 100k+, fractional GBP 1-3k/month (Weak, needs sourcing)

**So What?** Bad financial decisions at pre-seed and seed compound. Founders run out of cash earlier than they thought, raise at dilutive valuations because they cannot defend their numbers, and miss SEIS/EIS windows that cost them investors.

**Evidence Rating:** Moderate

**Gaps to validate:**
- Real quote from a founder, used with permission (upgrades Moderate to Strong)
- One specific horror story of a founder who lost money or missed a milestone due to bad finance work
- Verified cost benchmark for fractional CFO services in the UK

---

### Solution

**Key Narrative:** Ginger Bread gives UK B2B SaaS founders CFO-grade clarity from first revenue, so they can stop muddling through their finances and start running their company like a grown-up.

**Three outcome pairs (the Solution slide):**
1. **Visibility:** Know exactly how much money they are spending and where, instead of losing sleep at night guessing.
2. **Decisions:** Say yes to a new hire in the morning because they know to the day when the money runs out, instead of delaying for six weeks because they are too scared to run the numbers.
3. **Investor credibility:** Answer "what's your burn multiple?" in ten seconds without blushing, instead of muttering "I'll follow up by email" and losing the room.

**So What?** A pre-seed founder using Ginger Bread shows up to the Series A raise with the clarity of a company three stages ahead. That is a compounding advantage.

**Evidence Rating:** Moderate (grounded in discovery conversations, not yet validated by behavioural evidence)

### Market

**Key Narrative:** Ginger Bread starts with a ~GBP 9M UK wedge (B2B SaaS founders between first revenue and Series A), expands into a ~GBP 100M European opportunity at scale, and addresses a multi-billion dollar global market for AI-native finance operations.

**Bottoms-up sizing:**
- **SOM:** ~2,500 UK B2B SaaS companies at target stage x GBP 3,588/year = GBP 9M annual revenue opportunity. Realistic 3-year penetration: 5-10% = 125-250 customers = GBP 450k-900k ARR (Series A ready).
- **SAM:** ~20,000-30,000 European B2B SaaS companies at target stage = GBP 72-108M annual revenue.
- **TAM:** Global fractional CFO services ~$5-10bn, ripe for AI disruption. Global SMB finance software ~$50bn.

**So What?** A founder landing 250 UK customers at GBP 299/month hits ~GBP 900k ARR. That is a Series A-ready number achievable inside the UK wedge alone.

**Evidence Rating:** Assumption (all numbers need verification)

**Gaps to validate:**
- Beauhurst or Dealroom lookup for UK B2B SaaS company count at pre-seed/seed
- Five pricing conversations with target founders
- IBISWorld or Gartner report for fractional CFO market sizing

### Product

**Key Narrative:** Ginger Bread is an AI CFO that lives inside the founder's financial stack. Chat interface for questions, AI agents for execution, and a dashboard that earns its place as the agents build the picture. Live from day one with direct connections to the founder's actual bank accounts and their accounting system.

**True Today (Moderate):**
- Working prototype with direct API connectivity to UK bank accounts
- Integrations with both Xero and QuickBooks
- Natural language chat interface
- Persistent context across sessions (memory)

**Next Build, 6 months post-raise (Assumption):**
- Migration to TrueLayer for scalable multi-bank coverage
- Proactive anomaly detection and alerts
- SaaS-specific finance logic: MRR, burn multiple, deferred revenue, R&D tax credits, SEIS/EIS support

**Vision (Assumption):**
- Auditable, trustworthy AI that replaces the thinking work of a fractional CFO
- Dashboard built from real, reconciled data

**Differentiation (vs "just paste into ChatGPT"):**
- Integration depth: lives inside the financial stack, no copy-pasting
- Continuity: remembers every conversation, decision, and forecast
- (Planned) Proactive: tells you things you did not know to ask
- (Planned) SaaS-specific logic: understands deferred revenue, burn multiples, R&D tax credits

**Evidence Rating:** Moderate on current prototype, Assumption on planned features

### Business Model

**Key Narrative:** Monthly SaaS subscription, outcome-framed tiers that mirror the founder's journey.

| Tier | Outcome | Price | Who |
|------|---------|-------|-----|
| Survive | Know where your money is going and when it runs out | ~GBP 149/month | Solo founder, first revenue, simple stack |
| Control | Make confident financial decisions and stop leaving money on the table | ~GBP 299/month | Seed stage, growing team, investor reporting |
| Scale | Walk into your Series A with the financial clarity of a company three stages ahead | ~GBP 499/month | Series A, finance team forming |

**Margin story:** Traditional fractional CFO costs GBP 1-3k/month and does not scale (every client needs more human hours). Ginger Bread's marginal cost per customer is near zero once integrations and AI logic are built. 80%+ gross margin at scale.

**Expansion revenue:** Natural upgrade path from Survive to Control to Scale as customers grow. Net revenue retention could exceed 130%.

**Evidence Rating:** Assumption (pricing and tiers untested)

**Gaps to validate:**
- Test Survive/Control/Scale framing with 5 founders
- Validate GBP 149/month entry price against founder pain threshold

### Traction

**Key Narrative:** Pre-revenue, pre-launch. But Ginger Bread has something most pre-seed startups would kill for: a built-in distribution channel of 1,000 founders and 20 accelerator programmes full of the exact target customer.

**Assets:**
- 10 discovery conversations confirming core pain points (Moderate)
- Working prototype with live API integrations to UK banks, Xero, and QuickBooks (Moderate)
- Direct access to 1,000+ early-stage founders (Moderate)
- 20 accelerator programmes as embedded distribution channel (Strong)

**So What?** The path from prototype to 10 paying customers is not hypothetical. The first cohort of customers is already in the founder's network. The GBP 250k raise funds the product, not the distribution.

**Evidence Rating:** Moderate-Strong (distribution is the standout)

**Gap:** No behavioural evidence yet. Distribution advantage only valuable if the product converts. First 5 paying customers upgrade this entire slide from Moderate to Strong.

**Highest priority action:** Pick 5 founders from the network in the sweet spot (B2B SaaS, first revenue, doing their own finances). Show them the prototype. Ask if they would pay GBP 149/month. If three say yes, ask for a pilot commitment.

### Vision

**Key Narrative:** Three-act vision using the "False Peak" technique.

**Act 1 (Tool):** Ginger Bread becomes the default AI CFO for early-stage B2B SaaS founders across Europe. Every founder from first revenue to Series A runs their finances through us. We replace the fractional CFO market for startups.

**Act 2 (Data Layer, the real vision):** A thousand SaaS companies running their finances through Ginger Bread creates the most comprehensive live dataset on startup financial health in Europe. Who is growing. Who is struggling. Who needs funding. Who is about to run out of cash. That data layer turns Ginger Bread from a tool into infrastructure. The Bloomberg terminal for early-stage startup finance.

**Act 3 (Platform):** Payments, treasury, banking, investor reporting, cap table management. All flowing through one AI-native financial layer. The financial operating system for every startup that does not have a finance team.

**Flywheel:** More customers = better data = better product = more customers.

**Evidence Rating:** Assumption (vision by definition, flywheel logic is sound, precedent from Stripe and Xero)

### The Ask

**Key Narrative:** Raising GBP 250k pre-seed (SEIS-eligible). 24 months of runway. Camel startup philosophy: default alive, profitable by month 24, then raise from a position of strength.

**Use of funds:**
- **Build (50%, ~GBP 125k):** First engineering hire (part-time/contract). Core product: proactive alerts, SaaS-specific finance logic, TrueLayer migration, dashboard.
- **Sell (30%, ~GBP 75k):** Founder-led sales through existing network and 20 accelerator programmes. First 10 customers from warm pipeline, next 40-90 through referrals and programme partnerships.
- **Operate (20%, ~GBP 50k):** Founder salary, infrastructure, legal (incorporation, SEIS/EIS advance assurance), tools.

**Milestones:**
- Month 6: 10 paying customers, ~GBP 18k ARR
- Month 12: 30 customers, ~GBP 72k ARR
- Month 18: 60 customers, ~GBP 180k ARR
- Month 24: 100 customers, ~GBP 350k ARR. Seed-ready.

**Camel philosophy:** "We are not building a unicorn on day one. We are building a camel. Default alive, profitable by month 24, with 100 paying customers and a seed raise from a position of strength, not desperation. The unicorn becomes possible because the camel survives."

**Evidence Rating:** Assumption (financial projections need a model)

**Critical pre-raise actions:**
1. Incorporate the company
2. Apply for SEIS advance assurance (6-8 weeks from HMRC)
3. Build a simple financial model (monthly customer acquisition, churn, revenue, cash balance)

## Phase 2: Positioning Diagnostic

**Sequoia Archetype:** Hard Fact
The problem is known but normalised. Every founder knows their finances are a mess. They assume that is how it is until they can afford a CFO. Ginger Bread reframes what they have been accepting unnecessarily.

**Dunford Positioning:** Dominate a Subsegment
Not head-to-head with Xero or QuickBooks (accounting tools, not CFO thinking). Not creating a new category (AI CFO exists as a concept). Owning a niche nobody serves well: UK B2B SaaS founders between first revenue and Series A.

**Market Framing:** AI has made it possible to deliver CFO-grade thinking at software prices for the first time. The timing is now because LLMs + open banking APIs make this buildable by a domain expert. Two years ago this product was impossible. Two years from now someone else will have built it.

## Phase 3: Story Arc

**Selected arc:** Demo Day Arc with Dunford market insight move

**Flow (5-minute version):**
1. Hook (15s) - identity hook + absurdity punch
2. Problem (30s) - accidental CFO, failing at the job
3. Market insight (20s) - existing tools record numbers but do not think
4. Solution (30s) - three before/after pairs
5. Product (30s) - chat + agents + dashboard, live integrations
6. Market (25s) - bottoms-up UK wedge, European expansion, global TAM
7. Business Model (20s) - Survive / Control / Scale tiers
8. Traction (30s) - distribution moat: 1,000 founders, 20 programmes, working prototype
9. Team (30s) - the strongest slide, slow down and make eye contact
10. The Ask (25s) - GBP 250k, camel philosophy, 24-month milestones
11. Vision / Close (25s) - tool then data layer then platform, False Peak

**3-minute compressed version:**
Merge Market + Business Model into one "here is where we are" beat. Compress Product into Solution. Cut Market Insight. Shorten Team to one credential sentence plus the purity of motivation line.

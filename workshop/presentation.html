What are investors buying?

Not your product.

Not your market.

Not your deck.

You.

Early-stage investors are buying the founder. Your credibility, your domain expertise, your ability to execute.

Investor psychology

Two forces drive every decision

Greed

"I want to end up on the Midas List."

This is what makes them take the meeting.

Fear

"I do not want an embarrassing failure on my track record."

This is what makes them say no.

The currency of the relationship is TRUST.

Built through consistency, specificity, and credibility.

Slide deep dive

The Hook

Bad Hooks

"Hi, I am Arben and this is FieldFlow."
"Our mission is to digitise trades businesses."
"Let me start with a quick overview."

Good Hooks

"Last month, a plumber lost EUR 2,400 because three jobs fell through the cracks of his paper diary."
"87% of trades businesses have no digital tools. They are losing money every single day."
"What if invoicing took 30 seconds instead of 3 days?"
If your hook does not make someone look up from their phone, it is not a hook.

The Ask

Vague Asks

"We are looking for strategic partners and investment."
"We are raising money to grow."

Specific Asks

"We are raising EUR 150K to reach 50 paying customers in 12 months by hiring one engineer and running pilots in three cities."

Specific amount. Specific milestones. Specific timeline.

The Rules

Do

Hook first. Earn their attention before you explain anything.
Evidence on every slide. No claim without proof.
Specific ask. Amount, milestones, timeline.
Practise 5+ times. Out loud, with a timer.
Time yourself. Know your pace cold.
Make eye contact. Talk to people, not screens.

Do Not

Start with your logo. Nobody cares yet.
Read your slides. If you are reading, you are not presenting.
Apologise. No "we are early" or "this is rough".
Run over time. It signals you cannot prioritise.
Show metrics you do not have. Aspirational numbers destroy credibility.
Feature dump. Investors buy outcomes, not features.
